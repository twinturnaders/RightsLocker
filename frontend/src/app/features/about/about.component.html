Overview

About RightsLocker

RightsLocker preserves the integrity and privacy of photo and video evidence with a clean, export-friendly workflow.

What happens when you upload

  • Hashing (SHA-256): We compute a fingerprint for the original and each derivative. If a file changes, the hash changes.
  • Chain of custody: We log timestamps, versions, and actions to create a tamper-evident trail.
  • Transparent reporting: You can download a printable report listing files, sizes, hashes, and processing steps.
  • Privacy tools: Metadata stripping and redaction are available by default.

Ways to use it

Processing only

Upload, process, and download. Files are removed automatically after 24 hours.

Hosted locker

Retain originals and derivatives, enable legal hold, and manage access from your account.

Plain-English definitions

Hash: a one-way, fixed-length fingerprint of a file. Matching SHA-256 hashes show two files have identical contents.

Chain of custody: a chronological log of how a file was handled. It helps others independently verify integrity.
