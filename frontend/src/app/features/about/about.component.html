About RightsLocker

RightsLocker preserves the integrity and privacy of photo/video evidence.

What happens when you upload

  • Hashing (SHA-256): We compute a fingerprint for the original and each derivative. If even a single pixel changes, the hash changes.
  • Chain of custody: We log timestamps, versions, and actions to create a tamper-evident trail.
  • Transparent reporting: You can download a printable report listing files, sizes, hashes, and processing steps.
  • Privacy tools: Metadata stripping and redaction are available by default.

Ways to use it

Processing-only (default)

Upload → process → download. We auto-delete within 24 hours.

Hosted Locker (opt-in)

We retain originals + derivatives under your retention policy. You can enable legal hold and share links.

Plain-English definitions

Hash: a one-way, fixed-length fingerprint of a file. Matching SHA-256 hashes prove two files have identical contents.

Chain of custody: a chronological log of how a file was handled. It helps others independently verify integrity.
